Demystifying Lending
In just 5 minutes customers received notification for loan credit in the bank accounts after clicking a button. The experience felt like magic to most, while others were amazed it could be possible and simple.

Sterling Bank undertook an audacious step to demystify the jinx of Lending in Nigeria.

I was part of the project to deliver a simple, fast and desirable experience to the customer.

Problem Statement
Lending in Nigeria is characterized as a tedious process for customers and risky for lenders. Customers complaint revolves around the experience during loan applications due to the tacky structure within organizations.

Over the years, the challenge has loomed, organizations record profit decline annually and the customer recounts a poor experience.
The solution
Specta provided the opportunity of lending to customers a 5 minutes experience eliminating human factor delays and automating the process end to end without stress and hassles of documentation.

Outcomes

Usability testing
During the testing phase we received feedback sounding Credit Risk and credibility of Customer. This led to versioning of the product;

1. Specta for Salaried 2. Specta for non-salaried.
Versioning
Specta has evolved into a product with different variants. Specta for Salaried customers has disbursed more that N20 Billion in Loan with less than 1% NPL (non-performing loan).

Specta for individuals disbursed more than N10 Billion within 6 months since released. At the the moment, the returns are yet to be calculated.
Lesson learnt
Seeking Critical Feedback

When we began the project, we were blinded by our ambitions to build the first digital product. Also, due the non-availability of competition, we did not carry out enough user testing before launch.

This resulted in pre-launch iterations & technical issues encountered during initial release.

A better approach would be to have conducted live close user testing to gather feedback, iterate and get more feedback to confirm all needs, technical issues are resolved before release to the general public.
